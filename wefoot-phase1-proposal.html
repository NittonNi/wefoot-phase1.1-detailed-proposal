Context & Objective

Starting on
the pitch.

Following our recent exchanges, this proposal outlines the structure, scope and budget for Phase 1.1 of our collaboration — a focused regional validation before moving into a broader retail approach.

The goal is not to generate theoretical research. It is to put the product in front of real players and coaches, collect honest and structured feedback under real conditions, and build a foundation that makes Phase 1.2 — the retail approach — meaningfully stronger.

What This Phase Delivers
  • Real usage data from selected players across training and match conditions
  • Structured before/after comparison across comfort, grip, and performance
  • Direct input from 2–3 coaches on professional perception and fit
  • Market signals: purchase intent, price perception, switching likelihood
  • A validated foundation for the retail and go-to-market approach in Phase 1.2

Phase Structure — Reminder

  • Phase 1.1 — Regional Validation (this proposal)
  • Phase 1.2 — Market Validation / Retail Approach (to be defined based on Phase 1.1 results)

Scope

What we
will validate.

User Testing

14–18 Participants

Selected from professional and semi-professional environments across Spanish leagues. Structured around a core group for depth and an extended group to validate patterns.

  • Core (10–12): 1–2 full real-use sessions each
  • Extended (4–6): at least one structured session each
Evaluation Framework

3 Core Dimensions

Each participant assessed before first use (baseline) and after each session (post-use comparison).

  • Comfort — fit, feel, post-session perception
  • Stability & grip — on-pitch confidence
  • Overall performance vs current product
Expert Input

2–3 Coaches

Technical staff embedded in the testing environments. Short structured conversations — not surveys — focused on professional perception, observed player reactions, and fit within training methodology.

Market Signals — Collected Post-Testing
Purchase Intent

Would participants buy the product? At what price range? Collected via structured post-use question, not open-ended.

Perceived Value

How does Wefoot compare to what participants currently use? What would make them switch permanently?

Recommendation Likelihood

Would they recommend to teammates or other coaches? This is the clearest early signal for organic adoption potential.

Milestones

Four steps.
Seven weeks.

Milestone 1 — Setup & Test Design
Week 1–2
  • Selection and confirmation of participants and testing environments
  • Definition of the testing framework: what we validate, how feedback is collected, baseline and post-use question structure
  • Coordination of product distribution once shipment is received
  • Alignment call with Wefoot to confirm scope and any final adjustments
Milestone 2 — Testing Execution
Week 3–5
  • Product distributed to all participants
  • Testing sessions carried out across training sessions and match conditions
  • Baseline inputs and post-use feedback collected in structured format after each session
  • Ongoing field observations documented throughout
Milestone 3 — Analysis & Insight Consolidation
Week 6
  • All feedback structured and analysed across the three evaluation dimensions
  • Key patterns identified: what works, what doesn't, what stands out
  • Baseline vs post-use comparison completed for all participants
  • Draft conclusions and recommendations prepared for the final report
Milestone 4 — Final Report & Joint Review
Week 7
  • Final validation report delivered to Wefoot
  • Joint session to review results, key learnings and field conclusions
  • Definition of next steps and structure for Phase 1.2
